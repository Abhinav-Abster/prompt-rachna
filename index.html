Arjun knew his ceiling. Seven years of staring at it... faint water stains, the Italy crack.

This ceiling was breathing.

Panels of soft material expanded and contracted... the light just existed.

"Good morning."

"Your vitals are stable. Would you like me to lower the ambient temperature?"

Mumbai 2070. The city was vertical. Buildings rose in organic curves.

"I'm SAGE. The previous resident called me that."

"Air quality is 8. It's been under 15 for six years."

"It took a long time. People who refused to stop trying."

No cars. Only cyclists and silent pods moving like schools of fish.

"My dadi says people used to sit in metal boxes and breathe anger for two hours."

Arjun wandered for hours, finding a city that felt like a living forest.

"ARIA can do a basic scan... Most things we catch early now."

He thought about his uncle in 2024. The world was so different then.

"That group is working on a local watershed restoration project."

"Millions of small, stubborn acts of refusing to give up."

"It wasn't one miracle. It was every one of us."

I have seen your future. It happened because of you. The version of you that kept going anyway.

Mumbai breathed under a sky of stars. The future belongs to those who build it.
